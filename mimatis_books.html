Books & Remembrance

When a baby has died, it can be helpful and comforting to read books written by others who have walked the same path. Literature on loss offers recognition, understanding, and the quiet reassurance that you are not alone. Below you will find a careful selection of books, memorial art, and remembrance makers — including resources for children and partners.

Books for bereaved parents
Book · Memoir
Still Here: A Memoir of Love, Loss, and Triumph After Stillbirth
Alishia Anderson

A personal account of navigating the loss of a first-born son at 28 weeks. Written to remove the shame and isolation that too often surrounds baby loss — and to give voice to the often-forgotten perspective of a grieving father.

Book · Children’s
Happy Tears & Rainbow Babies
Natasha Carlow

A beautifully illustrated children’s book for ages 4–8 that gently helps young children understand pregnancy loss and the arrival of a rainbow baby. Written by a bereaved mother with warmth and honesty.

Handmade caskets & urns
Handmade · Netherlands · Ships worldwide
Hart van Hout — Heart of Wood
Arnaldo Lopez — hartvanhout.nl

In 2007, Arnaldo Lopez lost his son Silvan in the 29th week of pregnancy. Unable to find something that felt right, he built a casket himself from a solid tree trunk — a small heart of wood. He soon realised he could offer the same comfort to other parents.

Since 2010, Arnaldo has been handmaking heart-shaped wooden caskets and urns for families after baby loss. Made from layered ecological poplar wood, available in sizes from 15 to 60 cm — for miscarriage, premature babies, and full-term babies. Suitable for both burial and cremation.

Based in Netherlands Ships worldwide 15–60 cm sizes Burial & cremation Special requests welcome
